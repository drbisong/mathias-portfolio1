About Me

Highly experienced Veterinary Epidemiologist with over 10 years of experience in zoonotic disease surveillance and outbreak investigation. Trained in advanced field epidemiology with expertise in One Health and AMR control. Demonstrated success in leading outbreak responses for Mpox, Yellow fever, Lassa fever, Rabies, COVID-19 and Polio. Worked as Veterinary Epidemiologist on the monkeypox Animal Reservoir Surveillance Project in Nigeria and as Postgraduate Research Assistant with West Africa One Health Actions for Understanding, Preventing and Mitigating Outbreaks Project, Center for the Prevention and Control of Zoonoses, University of Ibadan, where the One Health concept and community-based participatory research methods are applied for the prevention, mitigation, and control of Mpox, Lassa fever, Ebola and COVID-19. Worked as assistant director for over 4 years at the Federal Ministry of Livestock Development, enhancing my ability to work under pressure with minimal supervision. Proven ability to adapt in high-pressure environment and to help my team achieve set objectives. My professional goal is to strengthen integrated surveillance systems at the human-animal-ecosystem interface and to contribute to evidence-based policy development that supports health system resilience and AMR control in West Africa.
